Our Zoo Animals

This catalogue lists all of our current zoo animals and a few necessary facts about them. Updated weekly.

Bears

Here are our bears at the zoo:

  1. Ollie
  2. Mona
We currently have two bears at our zoo. Ollie is our male grizzly and Mona is our female. Ollie tends to sleep all day and likes to do so in odd places, so don't worry if you find him sleeping in strange areas. Mona is relatively active but sometimes likes to dig pits and watch the fish pond silently. She does not like it when Ollie sleeps next to her when this happens. Provide each with a variety of fruits, vegetables, meat, nuts and seeds, and insects and honey. New enrichment items will be provided every other day.

Learn more about bears.

Giraffes

Here are our giraffes:

  1. Frankie
  2. Coconut
Our giraffes Frankie and Coconut are best friends! They tend to always stay together, even when eating or sleeping. Frankie likes to bow to Coconut. Both firaffes are fed a diet of fresh leaves, vegetables, and specially formulated giraffe pellets to ensure they have all the nutrients they need. They prefer snacks stuffed inside their hanging enrichment feeder toys. Coconut enjoys fresh coconut pieces while Frankie enjoys apples.

Learn more about giraffes.

Lions

Here are our lions:

  1. Mella
  2. Karl
We currently home two lions: Karl, who is a male, and Mella who is a female. Exciting news! Mella is currently pregnant and due soon! Beware: Karl is incredibly playful but does not know his strength. Mella is more mellow and prefers to rest in shaded areas. Both lions are provided with a balanced diet of fresh meat and supplements. Mella should be monitored daily as she nears her due date.

Learn more about lions.

Monkeys

Here are our monkeys:

  1. Cookie
  2. Earl
  3. Banana Pudding
We have three sibling monkeys: Cookie, Earl, and Banana Pudding. They are Lion-tailed macaques and are incredibly social. They love coming up to observe the visitors. They are not picky with their food and thus we provide them with a wide variety of fruits, flowers, leaves, insects, and fish. Beware: they have learned to team up to steal more snacks from the snack bucket.

Learn more about monkeys.

Alligators

Here are our American alligators:

  1. Wren
  2. Aspen
  3. Mika
We currently are home to three american alligators: Aspen, Mika, and Wren. Wren is our only albino of the zoo. He requires weekly brushing to remove the algae from his scutes and enjoys it very much! Aspen and Mika like to hang out at the docks together while Wren prefers to float around in the pool right under the waterfall. It is best to feed each individually as they all like to fight for food.
